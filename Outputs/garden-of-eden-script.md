## ACT 1: THE CREATION OF PARADISE (1:30 - 6:00)

### Scene 4 — Before the Garden
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** A vast, formless void — dark waters stretching endlessly under a sky with no stars. Then a single brilliant beam of divine white light piercing through the darkness. Hyper-realistic, cinematic, cosmic scale.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 1:1 — In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth."*

**Narration:**
In the beginning, there was nothing. No light. No land. No life. The earth was formless and void, and darkness covered the face of the deep. And then — God spoke. "Let there be light." And there was light. In six days, God shaped the entire universe from nothing. He separated light from darkness. He raised the land from the seas. He filled the skies with stars and the oceans with life. He created every animal, every bird, every creature that moves upon the earth. But God was not finished. Everything He had made so far — the galaxies, the mountains, the vast oceans — all of it was leading to this one moment. The creation of His masterpiece.

---

### Scene 5 — The Creation of Adam
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** God as a divine glowing presence, hands reaching down to form a man from red-brown clay dust on the ground. Golden divine light surrounding the scene. The first man taking shape from the earth. Cinematic, dramatic, biblical art style.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 2:7 — Then the Lord God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life."*

**Narration:**
Genesis chapter two, verse seven tells us something extraordinary. "Then the Lord God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul." Stop and think about that for a moment. Every other thing in creation — God spoke it into existence. He said the word and it appeared. But when it came to man, God did something different. He got down. He took the dust of the earth into His hands. And He shaped man — carefully, intentionally, personally. This was not a command from a distance. This was intimate. This was a Father forming His child. And then God did something He did for no other creature. He leaned in close, and breathed His own breath — His own life — into Adam's lungs. The Hebrew word used here is "neshamah" — the breath of God. Adam did not just receive air. He received the very essence of the divine.

---

### Scene 6 — The Planting of Eden
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** An absolutely stunning paradise garden with crystal-clear rivers, massive ancient trees bearing colorful fruits, lush green meadows with flowers of every color, golden sunlight filtering through the canopy. Animals peacefully coexisting — lions beside lambs, deer drinking from streams. Ultra-realistic, 8K cinematic, paradise on earth.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 2:8 — And the Lord God planted a garden eastward in Eden."*

**Narration:**
And then, God planted a garden. Not just any garden. The Scripture says God planted it "eastward, in Eden." The word "Eden" in Hebrew means "delight" or "pleasure." This was a place designed by God Himself for the pure delight of His creation. Imagine a place where every tree was beautiful to look at and produced the most perfect fruit you have ever tasted. Rivers of crystal-clear water flowed through the land, splitting into four great rivers — the Pishon, the Gihon, the Tigris, and the Euphrates. The ground was rich with gold, precious stones, and aromatic resin. Animals of every kind roamed freely with no fear, no violence, no death. There were no thorns. No weeds. No storms. No decay. The temperature was perfect. The air was pure. And the presence of God Himself walked among the trees. This was not just a garden. This was heaven on earth.

### Scene 7 — Adam's Purpose
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** Adam, a strong and noble man, standing among animals in the garden — naming them one by one. A majestic lion sits peacefully beside him, birds perch on his shoulders, deer approach without fear. Warm golden light, cinematic biblical art.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 2:15 — The Lord God took the man and put him in the Garden of Eden to work it and keep it."*

**Narration:**
God placed Adam in the garden with a purpose. Genesis 2:15 says God put him there "to work it and to keep it." Adam was not idle. He had purpose, responsibility, and authority. God brought every animal and every bird to Adam and gave him the extraordinary task of naming them. Whatever Adam called each living creature — that became its name. Think about what this means. God — the Creator of the universe — gave Adam authority over His creation. He trusted this man with dominion over the earth. Adam was not a servant. He was a son. A prince in paradise. Walking in perfect communion with God every single day. But even in this perfect place, God saw something that was not yet complete.

## ACT 2: THE WOMAN, THE TREES, AND THE COMMAND (6:00 - 12:00)

### Scene 8 — It Is Not Good
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** Adam standing alone in the garden at dusk, looking at pairs of animals walking together — two birds, two deer, two lions — while he stands by himself. A look of quiet longing on his face. Cinematic, emotional, warm but melancholic lighting.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 2:18 — The Lord God said, 'It is not good for the man to be alone.'"*

**Narration:**
Throughout the entire creation account, God looked at everything He made and said, "It is good." The light — good. The land — good. The animals — good. But now, for the first time, God says something is not good. "It is not good for the man to be alone." This is remarkable. Adam was in paradise. He had everything. He walked with God. He had authority over creation. And yet, God Himself identified a need that even paradise could not fill. Adam needed a companion. A partner. Someone who was bone of his bone and flesh of his flesh. Not beneath him like the animals. Not above him. But beside him.

---

### Scene 9 — The Creation of Eve
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** Adam lying in a deep sleep on soft grass in the garden, divine golden light surrounding him. A glowing, ethereal form of a woman being shaped from his side by an invisible divine hand. Mystical, cinematic, sacred atmosphere.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 2:21-22 — The Lord God caused a deep sleep to fall upon Adam... and He made a woman."*

**Narration:**
So God caused a deep sleep to fall upon Adam. And while he slept, God took one of his ribs — or as some scholars translate it, took from his side — and from it, He fashioned a woman. There is deep meaning in this. God did not take her from Adam's head, to rule over him. He did not take her from his feet, to be beneath him. He took her from his side — to walk beside him. From near his heart — to be loved by him. From under his arm — to be protected by him. When Adam awoke and saw her for the first time, he spoke the first recorded words of any human being in the Bible. "This is now bone of my bones and flesh of my flesh. She shall be called Woman, because she was taken out of Man." This was the first marriage. The first union. A bond created and blessed by God Himself in the perfect garden.

---

### Scene 10 — The Two Trees
**Duration:** 2 minutes
**AI Image Prompt:** Two magnificent trees standing in the center of the garden. One radiates warm, golden light with luminous fruit — the Tree of Life. The other is darker, mysterious, with an almost hypnotic beauty, bearing deep red fruit that seems to glow from within — the Tree of Knowledge. Dramatic contrast between light and shadow. Cinematic, foreboding, biblical.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 2:9 — The tree of life, and the tree of the knowledge of good and evil."*

**Narration:**
Now here is where the story takes a turn that many people overlook. In the middle of the garden, God placed two very specific trees. The first was the Tree of Life. This tree granted eternal life. As long as Adam and Eve had access to this tree, they would live forever in perfect communion with God. The second tree was the Tree of the Knowledge of Good and Evil. And about this tree, God gave one single command. One rule in all of paradise. Genesis 2:16-17: "You may freely eat of every tree of the garden, but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat, for in the day that you eat of it, you shall surely die."

Think about that. God gave them an entire paradise — thousands of trees, unlimited food, boundless freedom. And He asked only one thing. Do not eat from this one tree. This was not a trap. This was not God setting them up for failure. This was love. Because love — real love — requires a choice. Without the ability to choose, obedience means nothing. God did not want robots. He wanted relationship. He wanted Adam and Eve to choose Him — freely, willingly, because they trusted Him. That single tree represented the one thing that separated worship from slavery — free will.

## ACT 3: THE SERPENT AND THE FALL (12:00 - 18:30)

### Scene 11 — The Serpent Enters
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** A beautiful but unsettling serpent coiled around the branch of the forbidden tree. The serpent has an almost mesmerizing, elegant appearance — not ugly, but dangerously beautiful. Dark, moody atmosphere with shadows creeping into the garden. Red and amber tones. Cinematic, ominous.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 3:1 — Now the serpent was more cunning than any beast of the field."*

**Narration:**
And then — the enemy entered paradise. Genesis 3:1 says, "Now the serpent was more cunning than any beast of the field which the Lord God had made." The Bible tells us this was no ordinary animal. Revelation 12:9 identifies this serpent as "that ancient serpent called the devil, or Satan, who leads the whole world astray." Satan did not come into the garden with horns and fire. He came with beauty. He came with words. He came with a question — and that question was designed to do one thing: plant a seed of doubt. He approached Eve and said, "Did God really say you must not eat from any tree in the garden?" Notice the strategy. He did not start with a lie. He started with a distortion. He twisted God's words just enough to make Eve second-guess what she had been told. This is still his strategy today. He does not always attack with obvious evil. He attacks with subtle confusion.

---

### Scene 12 — The Temptation
**Duration:** 2 minutes
**AI Image Prompt:** Eve standing before the forbidden tree, reaching toward the glowing red fruit. The serpent is whispering near her ear. The fruit looks irresistibly beautiful. Light and shadow battle across the scene. Adam stands a short distance behind her, watching. Highly dramatic, cinematic tension, biblical art.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 3:4-5 — 'You will not surely die,' the serpent said. 'Your eyes will be opened, and you will be like God.'"*

**Narration:**
Eve answered the serpent. She said, "We may eat fruit from the trees in the garden, but God did say, 'You must not eat fruit from the tree that is in the middle of the garden, and you must not touch it, or you will die.'" Interestingly, Eve added something God never said. God never told them they could not touch the tree — only that they must not eat from it. This small addition may seem minor, but it reveals something important. When we add to God's word or take away from it, we become vulnerable to deception.

And then came the lie. The serpent said to the woman, "You will not surely die. For God knows that when you eat from it your eyes will be opened, and you will be like God, knowing good and evil." Three attacks in two sentences. First — he directly contradicted God. "You will not die." Second — he accused God of having selfish motives. He suggested God was keeping something good from them. Third — he appealed to pride. "You will be like God." This is the same sin that caused Lucifer himself to fall from heaven. The desire to be equal with God. And now, the same temptation was being placed before Eve.

---

### Scene 13 — The Fall
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** Eve biting into the forbidden fruit, juice dripping, with a look of both wonder and dawning horror. She hands the fruit to Adam who stands right beside her. The sky above begins to darken. Cracks of dark shadow spread across the garden like glass breaking. Ultra-dramatic, cinematic, the pivotal moment of human history.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 3:6 — She took of the fruit and ate it. She also gave some to her husband, who was with her, and he ate."*

**Narration:**
Genesis 3:6 records the most devastating moment in human history. "When the woman saw that the fruit of the tree was good for food and pleasing to the eye, and also desirable for gaining wisdom, she took some and ate it. She also gave some to her husband, who was with her, and he ate." Notice — Adam was right there. He was with her. He was not deceived by the serpent. First Timothy 2:14 tells us, "Adam was not the one deceived; it was the woman who was deceived and became a sinner." Adam ate with full knowledge of what he was doing. He chose Eve over God. And in that one act — that single bite — everything changed. Immediately, the Bible says, "the eyes of both of them were opened, and they realized they were naked." The innocence was gone. The purity was shattered. For the first time in human history, shame entered the world.

---

### Scene 14 — Hiding from God
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** Adam and Eve hiding behind trees and thick bushes, covered in crude fig leaves, looking terrified and ashamed. A brilliant, overwhelming divine light approaches through the garden. The atmosphere is heavy with sorrow. Cinematic, emotional, dramatic lighting contrast.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 3:8 — They hid themselves from the presence of the Lord God."*

**Narration:**
Then they heard the sound of the Lord God walking in the garden in the cool of the day. This tells us something beautiful and heartbreaking at the same time. God walked in the garden. This was routine. This was their daily fellowship — Creator and creation, Father and children, walking together in paradise. But this time, Adam and Eve did not run to meet Him. They ran from Him. They hid among the trees. And God called out to Adam, "Where are you?" God was not asking because He did not know. God is omniscient — He knows all things. He was asking because He wanted Adam to recognize where he was. Where sin had taken him. Away from God's presence. Away from the light. Hiding in shame and fear. And that is exactly what sin still does to us today. It makes us hide from the One who loves us the most.

## ACT 4: THE CONSEQUENCES AND THE PROMISE (18:30 - 23:00)

### Scene 15 — The Judgment
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** God's overwhelming divine presence confronting the serpent, which is now cursed and lowered to the ground. Adam and Eve kneel in sorrow behind. The garden begins to wither and fade at the edges. Lightning and divine fire in the sky. Epic, cinematic, judgment scene.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 3:14 — The Lord God said to the serpent, 'Cursed are you above all livestock and all wild animals.'"*

**Narration:**
God pronounced judgment. First upon the serpent: "Cursed are you above all livestock and all wild animals. You will crawl on your belly and you will eat dust all the days of your life." Then to Eve: "I will greatly increase your pain in childbearing. Your desire will be for your husband, and he will rule over you." And to Adam: "Cursed is the ground because of you. Through painful toil you will eat food from it all the days of your life. It will produce thorns and thistles for you. By the sweat of your brow you will eat your food until you return to the ground — for dust you are, and to dust you will return." Death had entered creation. The paradise was broken. The perfect world was fractured by a single act of disobedience.

---

### Scene 16 — The First Promise of Salvation
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** A powerful beam of divine light breaking through dark storm clouds above the garden, forming the subtle silhouette of a cross. The serpent recoils in the foreground. Adam and Eve look up with tears and hope. Cinematic, prophetic, deeply emotional.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 3:15 — He shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise his heel."*

**Narration:**
But hidden inside the curse upon the serpent is the most important promise in the entire Bible. God said to the serpent, "I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your offspring and hers. He will crush your head, and you will strike his heel." This is called the Protoevangelium — the first gospel. The first promise of a Savior. Right there in Genesis chapter three, in the middle of judgment and consequence, God revealed His plan of redemption. A descendant of the woman would one day come and crush the head of the serpent. Satan would strike His heel — meaning he would cause suffering — but the Savior would deliver the fatal blow. This is the first prophecy of Jesus Christ. Thousands of years before Bethlehem. Thousands of years before the cross. God already had a plan to save us.

---

### Scene 17 — God's Mercy: The First Sacrifice
**Duration:** 60 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** God lovingly clothing Adam and Eve in animal skins. A slain lamb lies nearby — the first blood sacrifice. Adam and Eve weep. The scene is tender yet devastating. Warm but sorrowful lighting. Cinematic, deeply emotional.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 3:21 — The Lord God made garments of skin for Adam and his wife and clothed them."*

**Narration:**
And then God did something that reveals His heart. Adam and Eve had covered themselves with fig leaves — their own attempt to cover their shame. But God replaced those leaves with garments made from animal skins. This means an animal had to die. Blood was shed for the first time in history. This was the first sacrifice. A foreshadow of every sacrifice that would follow — and ultimately, a foreshadow of the Lamb of God, Jesus Christ, whose blood would cover the sins of the world. Even in their punishment, God clothed them. Even in their failure, God provided for them. That is the heart of our God.

### Scene 18 — The Exile
**Duration:** 90 seconds
**AI Image Prompt:** Adam and Eve walking out of the Garden of Eden, looking back in anguish. Behind them, a massive angel — a Cherubim — stands at the garden entrance wielding a flaming sword that rotates in every direction. The garden glows with fading golden light behind the angel. The world outside is barren and grey. Epic, cinematic, heartbreaking.
**On-Screen Text:** *"Genesis 3:24 — He placed cherubim and a flaming sword flashing back and forth to guard the way to the tree of life."*

**Narration:**
Then the Lord God banished them from the Garden of Eden. He drove the man out, and at the east side of the garden, He placed cherubim — mighty angels — and a flaming sword flashing back and forth to guard the way to the Tree of Life. The door to paradise was closed. Adam and Eve stepped out into a world that was no longer perfect. A world of thorns, pain, labor, and death. They could never return. The Tree of Life was now guarded. Humanity was separated from God. And the world as we know it — with all its beauty and all its brokenness — began.
