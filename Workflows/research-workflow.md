# Research Workflow — Content Creator Edition

## Purpose
Use this workflow whenever you need to research a topic for YouTube or TikTok content.

**How to trigger it:** Say "Run the research workflow on [topic]"

---

## STEP 1 — Ask Clarifying Questions

Do this FIRST. Do not start researching yet.

Ask all of the following questions in a single message, then wait for the user's answers before moving on.

---

**Before I start researching [topic], I have a few quick questions:**

1. **Who is your target audience?**
   (Examples: beginners, teenagers, fitness people, entrepreneurs, general public, parents)

2. **What is the main goal of this content?**
   (Examples: educate, entertain, spark debate, go viral, build trust, promote something)

3. **Which platform is this for — YouTube, TikTok, or both?**
   - YouTube = longer, more in-depth, can handle nuance and detail
   - TikTok = fast hooks, punchy facts, short attention spans

4. **How deep should the research go?**
   - **Surface** — quick overview, 5-7 key facts, good for a short-form video
   - **Standard** — full breakdown, multiple angles, good for a 10-15 min YouTube video
   - **Deep Dive** — thorough analysis, stats, expert views, good for a documentary-style video

5. **Are there any angles, opinions, or subtopics you specifically want covered or avoided?**

6. **Do you have a video format in mind?**
   (Examples: listicle, explainer, story-driven, reaction, talking head, debunking myths)

---

Wait for the user to answer all questions before proceeding to Step 2.

---

## STEP 2 — Show Your Research Plan

After receiving answers, display a short plan before starting research. Do not begin researching yet — wait for the user to say "go" or confirm the plan.

Format it like this:

---

**Topic:** [topic]
**Platform:** [platform]
**Audience:** [audience]
**Depth:** [Surface / Standard / Deep Dive]
**Research angle:** [one sentence describing the lens you will research through]

**I will look into:**
- [subtopic 1]
- [subtopic 2]
- [subtopic 3]
- [subtopic 4]
- [subtopic 5]

Does this look right? Say "go" and I'll start researching.

---

Wait for confirmation before moving to Step 3.

---

## STEP 3 — Conduct Research

Use WebSearch and WebFetch to research the topic thoroughly.

**Search strategy — run at least 5 searches using different phrasings:**
- "[topic] explained"
- "[topic] statistics 2024 OR 2025"
- "[topic] myths debunked"
- "[topic] YouTube viral"
- "[topic] expert opinion"
- "[topic] [audience type] guide"
- "[topic] reddit" (for real audience questions)
- "[topic] latest news 2025"

**Reading sources — use WebFetch to read full pages, not just search snippets:**
- Collect at least 6-8 distinct sources before writing the report
- Prioritize: news outlets, academic or research sites, YouTube creators who cover this topic, Reddit threads, and official sources
- Avoid relying on a single website or perspective

**While researching, track:**
- Key facts and stats — note the source URL next to each one as you go
- Surprising or counterintuitive findings
- Questions the audience is already asking about this topic
- What video content already exists on this topic (what has performed well)
- Potential hook lines (an opening line that grabs attention in the first 3 seconds)

**Writing rules for uncertain claims:**
- If a claim cannot be verified from a reliable source, flag it with [UNVERIFIED] rather than stating it as fact

---

## STEP 4 — Write the Report

Read the template at resources/report-template.md and fill in every section.

**Writing rules:**
- Bullet points only — no paragraphs
- Plain English — no jargon unless the audience expects it
- Every stat or claim must include an inline citation: (Source: URL)
- Flag unverified claims with [UNVERIFIED]
- Content Angles section: provide 3 specific video ideas based on the research
- Hook Ideas section: provide 3 ready-to-use opening lines
- Key Takeaways section: summarize the most important conclusions and give concrete recommended next steps

---

## STEP 5 — Save the Report

Save the finished report to the Outputs/ folder.

**File naming format:**
research-[topic-slug]-[YYYY-MM-DD].md

A topic slug is the topic in lowercase with spaces replaced by hyphens, no special characters.

**Examples:**
- research-intermittent-fasting-2025-03-24.md
- research-passive-income-apps-2025-03-24.md
- research-75-hard-challenge-2025-03-24.md

**After saving, tell the user:**
- The file name and where it was saved
- How many sources were used
- The single most surprising finding from the research
